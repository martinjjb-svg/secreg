Securitisation Regulation

Certificate of compliance

{{heading}}

The transaction complies with the requirements of the applicable Securitisation Regulations.

The transaction characteristics meet the definition of a securitisation for the purposes of the Regulations

  • The transaction is a transaction or scheme where the credit risk associated with an exposure or pool of exposures is tranched;
  • Payments in the transaction/scheme are dependent upon the performance of the exposures or of the pool of exposures;
  • The subordination of the tranches determines the distribution of losses during the ongoing life of the transaction or scheme;
  • The transaction does not constitute a specialised lending transaction of the type set out in Article 147(8) of Reg (EU) No 575/2013 (The Capital Requirements Regulation);

The parties to the transaction are subject to the Securitisation Regulations

  • A relevant entity being the Sponsor, Originator or SSPE is within the jurisdiction.
  • Any investors within the jurisdiction will require the securitisation to comply.

The Risk Retainer is eligible for the purposes of the Securitisation Regulation being one of the following

  • sponsor - certain credit institutions or investment firms as specified in the Securitisation Regulations
  • original lender - entity directly or indirectly concluding the agreement for the underlying obligations
  • limb (a) originator - entity directly or indirectly, involved in the original agreement which created the obligations
  • limb (b) originator - entity which purchases a third party’s exposures on its own account and then securitises them
  • multi-originator 3(4)(a) - multiple originators / multiple original lenders pro rata to the exposures created by them
  • multi-originator 3(4)(b) - single originator/original lender (i) which established/managing ABCP programme/securitisation or (ii) established ABCP programme/securitisation and contributed more than 50% of securitised exposures

The Risk Retainer is not established for the sole purpose of the securitisation

  • The entity has a business strategy and the capacity to meet payment obligations consistent with a broader business enterprise
  • The responsible decision makers have the required experience to enable the entity to pursue the established business strategy, as well as having an adequate corporate governance arrangement in place.
  • The entity is not a special purpose vehicle established for the purpose of securitising the current exposures and the responsible decision makers are actively pursuing a business strategy which is beyond the securitisation of the relevant exposures.
  • The entity has access to capital, fees and revenue in addition to that rising from the securitisation of the current exposures.

The Risk Retainer holds and has agreed to maintain a material net economic interest in the securitisation as follows:

  • 5% of nominal value of each tranche sold; or
  • 5% of nominal value of each of the securitised exposures (revolving securitisations or revolving exposures); or
  • randomly selected exposures, equivalent to not less than 5% of nominal value of the securitised exposures; or
  • first loss tranche (or tranches with same of more severe risk profile) of at least 5%; or
  • first loss exposure of not less than 5% of every securitised exposure.

The parties have agreed as follows in accordance with the Regulations

  • The originator or original lender grants all the credits giving rise to the underlying exposures on the basis of sound and well-defined criteria and clearly established processes for approving, amending, renewing and financing those credits and has effective systems in place to apply those criteria and processes, (i) where established in the EU, in accordance with Article 9(1) of the EU Securitisation Regulation or (ii) where the originator or original lender is established in a third country, in order to ensure that credit-granting is based on a thorough assessment of the obligor’s creditworthiness.
  • Details of the Risk Retainer and the nature of the ongoing risk retention is disclosed to institutional investors in accordance with Article 7 of the Securitisation Regulation.
  • The originator, sponsor and SSPE have designated one party as the reporting entity and that entity will make certain prescribed information relating to the Transaction available to investors, competent authorities and, upon request, to potential investors.
  • Information that will be made available to investors by way of ongoing reporting obligations includes quarterly portfolio level disclosure (“Asset Reports”) and quarterly investor reports (“Investor Reports”) inside information relating to the securitisation that the reporting entity is obliged to make public under the Market Abuse Regulation (“Inside Information”), and any significant events (“Significant Events”) in accordance with the requisite detailed disclosure templates.
  • The securitisation has been, or will be filed with the relevant Securitisation Repository using the standardised reporting format.

Certificate

Based on the information provided and in accordance with the analysis above the securitisation satisfies the risk retention requirements set out in the Regulations.
