Securitisation Regulation

Compliance with requirements.

This guide determines if your transaction is a securitisation and if it is subject to the requirements set out within the EU Securitisation Regulation or the UK Securitisation Regulation or both. If the transaction is subject to the Regulations the guide takes you through the process of determining if it is compliant with the current Regulations.

Securitisation Regulation refers to the application of the Regulation (EU) 2017/2402 of the European Parliament and of the Council in respect of securitisations in the EU and the UK Securitisation Regulation in respect of securitisations in the UK (together the "Regulations").

Securitisation relates to a form of financing whereby an entity ("originator" or "sponsor") establishes a structure in which financial claims, usually in the form of rights to payment of an outstanding debt ("receivables") are transferred from the originator or original lender, usually through a sale ("true sale") to a company or other legal entity set up for the purpose of holding the claims ("securitisation special purpose entity" or "SSPE"). The SSPE aggregates the assets into a collateral pool ("exposures") and then issues notes to fund the purchase. The notes are tranched into tiers with the highest tier having first claim on the payments arising from the exposures and the second tier and subsequent tiers being subordinated to each higher tier, therefore having the next or subsequent claim after higher claims are satisfied until payments from the pool of exposures is exhausted. The default and expected loss rates applicable to the exposures determine the amount of subordination that is required in order to achieve a relevant credit rating applied to the respective tranche.

Typically, this approach is used in relation to receivables such as residential loans, auto loans, credit cards, leases, consumer loans and in certain cases various types of business or commercial assets.

This form of financing is subject to a number of regulatory requirements. In order to confirm if the regulatory requirements are complied with it is possible to follow the procedure set out below.

1. Determine if the transaction or scheme is a securitisation within the regulatory definition.

2. If it is a securitisation determine if the originator, sponsor or SSPE are subject to the Regulations.

3. Consider where the investors are based and if they are subject to the Regulations based on the jurisdiction of the relevant investors.

4. If the securitisation is subject to the Regulations check that it complies with the applicable regulatory requirements requiring an appropriate entity to retain a required net material economic interest in the securitisation.

5. Check that the risk retention entity complies with eligibility requirements applicable nder the Regulations.

6. Confirm the form of risk retention instrument and that this complies with relevant requirements.

7. Confirm reporting entity and compliance with credit granting and transparency and reporting requirements.

8. If securitisation meets all above requirements and information input into this site is correct, subject to verification and normal assumptions and reservations the regulatory risk retention requirements are complied with to the extent of the available guidance.

9. To review definitions or start analysis click on the Start Analysis button on the navigation bar at the top of this page
